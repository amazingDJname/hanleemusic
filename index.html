    Han geul Lee is a pianist, composer, teacher, improviser, instrument builder, and performer/producer of electronic music from Seoul, Korea. Having already studied and performed a wide breadth of piano repertoire including some of the most difficult and obscure, he continues to explore the untapped sonic capabilities of his piano with extended techniques and electronics - creating both original and collaborative works.

    A 2022 Fromm Foundation Fellowship recipient, Han is currently in close collaboration with select composers to commission new piano works involving live electronics. Also active as an electroacoustic composer himself, Han’s digital output ranges from experimental instrumental and acousmatic works to hip-hop, dance music as well as installation works, having premiered and presented multimedia projects at SUNY Stony Brook, Brandeis University, and at the University of Nottingham (UK) as well as having presented an interactive sound installation at the inaugural sound-art installation exhibit at the Mitzi Newhouse Pavilion of the Manhattan School of music.

    Despite having begun piano lessons at age of fourteen, he has won numerous competitions and awards including a Grand Prize awarded by the consul general consulate of the Republic of Korea. Since then he has performed chamber and solo recitals across the United States and Europe, and has been invited to study at the Manhattan School of Music under the President’s Award scholarship throughout multiple advanced degree programs in both classical performance and contemporary classical performance, while also having been active as a pianist/performer of TACTUS ensemble ("Ambitious, finely polished, spirited” —Allan Kozinn, The New York Times) Han geul has studied classical piano with Dr. Peter Mack, Dr. Enrico Elisi, Dr. Joanne Polk, Mr. Phillip Kawin, and contemporary music performance with Dr. Margaret Kampmeier, Mr. Anthony de Mare, and Dr. Christopher Oldfather.
